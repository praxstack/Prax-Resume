What is a cover note — and three you can paste.

A cover note is not a cover letter. It’s 4–6 lines that ride along with your resume so the recruiter knows why you’re reaching out, in 20 seconds.

Cover Note vs Cover Letter — what’s the difference?

Cover NoteCover Letter
Length4–6 lines, ~80 words3 paragraphs, ~300–400 words
Where it livesEmail body, LinkedIn DM, form’s “anything else?” fieldAttached PDF, separate document
ToneConversational, direct, no fluffFormal, structured, narrative
GoalGet them to open the resumeTell a story before they see the resume
Use it whenModern tech roles, cold outreach, referrals, follow-upsLegal, gov, traditional corporate, when explicitly asked
For UnifyApps Sr. FDSE: they want operators, not essayists. Send a cover note, not a cover letter. If their form has an open-text box you fill it with the medium version below. If you DM a recruiter you use the short version. If you’re emailing your resume directly, use the email version.

The structure that works

A good cover note has 4 jobs — in this exact order:

  1. 1 Hook — one sentence on the role/post and why you read it.
  2. 2 Proof — one or two lines with a name (Amazon), a number (47% p90 cut), and a public artifact (the Redis repo).
  3. 3 Disclosure (only if needed) — the form’s grad-year dropdown didn’t include 2022. Owning it upfront.
  4. 4 The ask — one specific, low-friction next step. “Worth a 15-min screen?” not “please consider me.”

Common mistakes to avoid

  • “I am writing to apply for the position of...” — cut. They know.
  • “I am a passionate, hardworking individual...” — cut. Show, don’t tell.
  • Restating the resume in paragraphs. The cover note’s job is to make them open the resume, not replicate it.
  • No specific number. If the note has zero numbers it sounds like every other note. 47%, 6 APIs, 154k rps — pick one.
  • No ask. Recruiters reply when there’s a clear next step. “15-min screen?” works. “Looking forward to hearing from you” doesn’t.
  • Hiding the grad-year discrepancy. If they figure it out later, you look like you tried to slip something past them. Owning it upfront looks like rigour.

How to actually send it

  1. If you have a recruiter email: use Version A in the email body. Attach the resume as PDF named Prakhar Parthasarthi — Sr FDSE.pdf (no brackets, no version numbers).
  2. If you only have the Google Form: the form may not have an open-text field. If it does, paste Version B. If it doesn’t, hold Version A in your clipboard and email it to careers@unifyapps.com (or whatever address you find) 30 minutes after submitting the form, referencing your form submission.
  3. If you’re DMing on LinkedIn: Version C. Wait for a reply before sending the resume link.
  4. For follow-ups: never resend the cover note. Send a 1-line bump: “hi [name], floating this back up — happy to send a 2-min walkthrough of the agentic engine if useful.”
One last thing: the note isn’t the document. The resume is the document. The cover note is the door-knock that gets the recruiter to open the resume. Keep that frame.
